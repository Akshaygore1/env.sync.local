dotenvx vs env-sync

Quick take: dotenvx is built around encrypted dotenv files and runtime env injection, while env-sync is built around syncing secrets across peer machines on a local network without a central control plane.

What each product optimizes for

dotenvx focuses on developer and CI workflows where `.env` files are encrypted, committed, and injected at runtime via CLI commands. It emphasizes cross-language support and multi-environment dotenv management.

env-sync focuses on host-to-host consistency in local networks. It discovers peers, pulls secrets over SSH or mTLS (depending on mode), merges changes, and keeps machines aligned without central SaaS dependencies.

When teams usually choose one over the other

  • Choose dotenvx when your pain is secure `.env` distribution/injection across app runtimes and CI steps.
  • Choose env-sync when your pain is multi-host drift and you need LAN-first, peer-to-peer synchronization with explicit trust modes.
  • Some teams combine both: dotenvx patterns inside app runtime, env-sync for cross-machine parity in local infrastructure.

Research notes (March 2026)

Current dotenvx messaging emphasizes secure dotenv workflows from the creator of dotenv, encrypted env files, and CLI-driven runtime injection. env-sync remains intentionally scoped to distributed machine sync with explicit operation modes for trust boundaries.
